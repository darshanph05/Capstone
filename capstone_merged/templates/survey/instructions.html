Perspective-taking and Spatial Orientation
This is an activity to understand your ability to imagine different perspectives or orientations in space. In this activity, you will see a picture consisting of seven objects. An example is shown below.
You will be asked to imagine that you are standing at one object and facing another object. Then you will be asked to point to another object. For example, imagine you are standing at the flower and facing the tree. Point to the cat.
In the next step, you will need to represent the position you are being asked to start from, the position of the object you are facing and the object you are pointing to in a circle.
  • Your position will be the centre of the circle.
  • The object you are facing will be at the top of the circle
  • An arrow will be drawn for you from the centre of the circle (i.e., the place where you are standing at) to the object you are facing
Your task is to draw another arrow from the centre of the circle to the object you are pointing to.
Example:
Imagine you are standing at the flower and facing the tree. Point to the cat.
In this item you are asked to imagine that you are standing at the flower. So, the flower will be the centre of the circle. Since you are facing the tree, it will be represented at the top of the circle. Your task is to draw an arrow pointing to the cat. In this example, the arrow has been drawn for you. Can you see that if you were at the flower facing the tree, the cat would be in this direction?
There are 12 problems in this task, one on each page. You have 10 minutes to work out all the problems in this task.
Try to drag the arrow to the correct directions but do not spend too much time on any one question.
If you find a problem hard, it is better to leave it and move to the next problem.
